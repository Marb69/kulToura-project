adobo

Adobo is one of the most iconic and beloved Filipino dishes, known for its savory, slightly tangy flavor. It is made by marinating meat — usually pork, chicken, or both in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns, then simmered until tender. Adobo can be dry or saucy, and is considered a cultural treasure that reflects the resourcefulness and flavor preference of the Filipino palate.

Brief History

The term ‘adobo’ comes from the Spanish word for ‘marinade’ or ‘seasoning,’ but the Filipino version existed even before colonization, using vinegar and salt as preservation methods. Over time, soy sauce was introduced by Chinese traders, enriching the flavor. Today, adobo remains a staple in Filipino kitchens and a symbol of culinary identity.
