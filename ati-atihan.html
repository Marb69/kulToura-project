Ati-Atihan

The Ati-Atihan Festival is held every January in Kalibo, Aklan, in honor of the Santo Niño (Child Jesus). Known as the “Mother of all Philippine Festivals,” it features tribal dancing, body paint, and rhythmic drumbeats with participants shouting “Hala Bira!” in the streets. The festival’s name means “to be like the Ati,” paying tribute to the island’s indigenous Ati people. It blends religious devotion with indigenous culture, making it a vibrant, spiritual, and historical celebration.

Brief History

Ati-Atihan began as a native celebration to honor the Ati people, who were believed to have helped the early Malay settlers in Panay. When Spanish missionaries arrived, it was re-framed as a festival for the Santo Niño. Over the years, it has retained its tribal roots while becoming a major religious and cultural celebration — inspiring other festivals like Sinulog and Dinagyang.
