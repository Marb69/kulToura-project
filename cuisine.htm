Filipino Cuisine

Lechon

A centerpiece of Filipino feasts, lechon is a whole roasted pig with crispy skin and juicy meat. Often served during fiestas and celebrations, it embodies Filipino hospitality and love for grand gatherings.

Pancit

Symbolizing long life and good health, pancit is a stir-fried noodle dish with veggies, meat, or seafood. Commonly served at birthdays, it reflects the Chinese influence in Filipino cuisine and culture.

Pork-Sinigang

Known for its sour tamarind broth, pork sinigang is a comforting dish loved for its perfect balance of savory and tangy flavors. It reflects the Filipino preference for hearty, soupy meals that bring families together.

Adobo

Arguably the most iconic Filipino dish, adobo is made by braising meat in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. It showcases the Filipino knack for bold flavors and practical preservation techniques.
