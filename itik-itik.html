Itik-itik

Itik-Itik is a playful and graceful Filipino folk dance that imitates the movements of a duck (itik in Bisaya). Dancers mimic waddling, flapping, and splashing motions as if swimming in rice paddies or shallow waters. Originating from Surigao in Mindanao, Itik-Itik reflects Filipino creativity, joy, and a deep connection with nature and daily rural life.

Brief History

Itik-Itik originated in Cantilan, Surigao del Sur, when a dancer named Kanang improvised duck-like movements during a baptism celebration. The dance grew popular for its charm and simplicity, blending nature mimicry with Filipino grace. Today, Itik-Itik is performed nationwide as a symbol of joy and ingenuity rooted in local storytelling.
