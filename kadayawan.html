kadayawan

Kadayawan is Davao City’s premier festival, held every August as a thanksgiving celebration for a bountiful harvest, peace, and the richness of indigenous culture. The name “Kadayawan” comes from the Mandaya word “madayaw,” meaning good, valuable, or beautiful. The festival honors Davao’s 11 ethnolinguistic groups, featuring colorful street dances, floral floats, indigenous rituals, and a strong spirit of unity among Lumad, Moro, and settlers.

Brief History

Kadayawan Festival began as Apo Duwaling in the 1980s, named after Mt. Apo, durian, and waling-waling orchid. It was transformed into Kadayawan to emphasize gratitude and respect for the 11 tribes of Davao. Today, it stands as a beacon of diversity, showcasing the vibrant cultures, dances, music, and bounty of Mindanao.
