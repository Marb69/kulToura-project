Kulintang

The kulintang is an ancient traditional musical instrument from Mindanao, composed of a row of small, horizontally-laid gongs played melodically. Prominent among the Maguindanaon, Maranao, and other Southern Philippine ethnic groups, the kulintang ensemble represents the rich cultural heritage of pre-Hispanic Filipino music. Played during ceremonies, celebrations, and storytelling, its rhythms reflect social structure, tradition, and community spirit.

Brief History

The kulintang tradition predates Spanish colonization and belongs to the wider gong-chime musical culture of Southeast Asia. In the Philippines, it is practiced among various Muslim and Lumad groups in Mindanao. Once central to courtship, celebrations, and status rituals, kulintang music is now a vital cultural heritage, preserved through teaching, festivals, and cultural revival movements.
