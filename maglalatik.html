maglalatik

Maglalatik is a high-energy Filipino folk dance performed by male dancers using coconut shell halves attached to their bodies. Originating from Biñan, Laguna, this mock-war dance depicts a battle between the Christian and Moro warriors over prized coconut meat (latik). With rapid drum-like tapping, acrobatic moves, and synchronized body slaps, Maglalatik is both a cultural performance and a display of martial agility.

Brief History

Maglalatik originated from Laguna and dramatizes a fight between Christian and Muslim groups over ‘latik,’ the coconut residue used in native sweets. The dance features mock battle scenes, divided into four parts — two war segments and two reconciliation parts. Traditionally performed by men, Maglalatik combines rhythm, drama, and athleticism rooted in local life and colonial history.
