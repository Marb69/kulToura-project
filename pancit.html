Pancit

Pancit is a beloved noodle dish in the Philippines, often prepared during celebrations, fiestas, and daily meals. It comes in many forms—saucy or dry, stir-fried or soupy and is made with different kinds of noodles and toppings depending on the region.

Brief History

Pancit, meaning “noodles” in Hokkien, was introduced by Chinese traders but has become uniquely Filipino through regional varieties like Pancit Malabon, Pancit Bihon, and Pancit Canton. It’s traditionally served during birthdays as a sign of long life and prosperity.
