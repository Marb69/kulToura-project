singkil

Singkil is a regal and dramatic traditional dance of the Maranao people from Mindanao. Performed by a female dancer gracefully stepping between crisscrossed bamboo poles, it narrates a scene from the epic “Darangen,” particularly the story of Princess Gandingan caught in an earthquake in the forest. Accompanied by royal music, fan movements, and ornate attire, Singkil showcases elegance, cultural heritage, and the richness of Mindanaoan tradition.

Brief History

Singkil comes from the Maranao people of Lanao del Sur and dramatizes a scene from the pre-Islamic epic Darangen, recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage. Traditionally performed by royalty or noblewomen, Singkil highlights status, grace, and heritage. It later evolved into a grander theatrical performance, often including male warriors, fans, and parasols.
