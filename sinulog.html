Sinulog

Sinulog is one of the grandest festivals in the Philippines, held every January in Cebu City to honor the Santo Niño (Child Jesus). It blends indigenous dance rituals with Spanish Catholic traditions, symbolizing the Filipino people's journey from paganism to Christianity. The dance features two steps forward and one step back mimicking the flow of a river and is performed in vibrant costumes, to the rhythmic beat of drums, trumpets, and native gongs.

Brief History

Sinulog traces back to pre-colonial times when natives danced to honor their anito (spirits). After the arrival of Christianity, the dance was re-offered to the Santo Niño — gifted by Ferdinand Magellan in 1521. Over time, Sinulog became a symbol of Cebuano devotion and identity. Today, it unites faith, history, and creativity in one of Asia’s most colorful celebrations.
