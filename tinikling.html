Tinikling

Tinikling is one of the most iconic traditional Filipino folk dances, known for its lively rhythm and graceful movements. Dancers skillfully step between two bamboo poles that are rhythmically clapped together, mimicking the movements of the tikling bird as it navigates through grass and obstacles. Originating from the Visayas, particularly Leyte, tinikling represents resilience, creativity, and harmony in motion.

Brief History

Tinikling originated from Leyte during the Spanish colonial era. The dance imitates the movements of the tikling bird as it escapes bamboo traps set by farmers. Historically, it was a way of expressing resistance and grace under pressure. Today, it is taught in schools and performed at festivals to celebrate Filipino creativity and rhythm.
