torogan

The Torogan is the traditional royal house of the Maranao people in Mindanao. Built entirely from hardwood and resting on wooden columns, it served as the residence of a datu or sultan. Its most iconic feature is the panolong the ornate, wing-like carvings on the beams, often adorned with okir designs. More than a home, the Torogan was a cultural symbol of status, artistry, and identity in pre-Islamic and Islamic Mindanao society.

Brief History

The Torogan was more than a home it was the center of Maranao power, diplomacy, and ritual. Its construction required skillful carpenters who could join massive beams without nails.
